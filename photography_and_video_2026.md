# HairMNL · Photography &amp; Video (2026 Addendum)

> This extends the 2020 Imagery guide. Core composition, lighting, and post rules are unchanged. What follows covers the new surfaces — vertical video, UGC, and creator-led content — that didn't exist (or didn't matter to us) five years ago.

---

## 1. The framing shift

In 2020 our imagery was mostly **editorial stills**, shot horizontally, leaning on white space.

In 2026, 60%+ of our impressions are **vertical video** (Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, IG Stories). Every concept gets a **9:16 first** treatment, with 1:1 and 16:9 crops derived from the same shoot.

Plan every shoot with three aspect-ratio safe areas on the monitor:
- **9:16** — the true frame.
- **1:1** — centre-cropped for IG feed.
- **16:9** — centre-cropped for YouTube.

Never shoot 16:9 and hope to re-crop later. It reads as afterthought.

---

## 2. Colour and light, still

- **Natural daylight + bounce card** is still our baseline. No ring light, ever. Ring light reads "influencer," not "editor."
- **Soft, warm shadows.** A single light-source, never two. If a shoot needs fill, use paper or linen, never another LED.
- **Warm-neutral grade.** The paper, stone, honey, and blush tones from the palette guide the LUT. Avoid cool blue grades even if the shoot is in a kitchen with cool tubes overhead — fix it in post.
- **Never oversaturate hair colour.** Real hair is chromatic, not Photoshop chromatic.

---

## 3. Vertical video — the rules

### Composition
- Subject held between the **upper and lower thirds**. Leave dead-centre clear — that's where the chrome (caption, username, share button) lands in every feed.
- Safe-text zone: **stay within the middle 72% vertically.** The top 14% gets clipped by the story UI; the bottom 14% by the caption + CTA.
- One subject in frame. If you must have two (GRWM, collabs), stack them vertically, not side-by-side.

### Motion
- **Cuts come from the hair**, not the music. Lead the cut with a flip, a brush, a twist — movement in the subject, not a snap-zoom on a flat shot.
- Cross-dissolves are banned. Hard cuts and match cuts only.
- Speed ramps: allowed once per video, in service of a hair movement. Never for emphasis on copy.

### Sound
- **Captions are default-on.** Always hard-sub in HairMNL typography (display for the hook, sans for the body). Don't rely on YouTube auto-captions.
- **No trending audio for owned content.** Trending audio works for TikTok-only posts but never cross-posts to Reels or YouTube. We will re-mix dialogue for each platform before we chase a trend.
- Original score (licensed, instrumental, warm) is the default for everything else.

---

## 4. UGC &amp; creator content

UGC is real customers; creator content is paid collaborations. Both are welcome. Neither gets a free pass on our brand floor.

### Floor
1. **Daylight only.** No ring light, tube, or coloured gels. If it's clearly a ring-lit bathroom selfie, we don't repost it.
2. **In focus.** No motion blur on a static face.
3. **Real hair.** No filters that smooth skin or change hair colour. A LUT is fine; a beauty filter is not.
4. **One product, one shot.** If the video is about four products, we re-cut it into four posts.
5. **No voiceover-over-trending-audio.** One or the other.

### Required ingredients for creator briefs
- The hair problem we're speaking to (not the product)
- Three talking points (what to mention, what *not* to mention)
- Aspect ratios required (9:16, plus 1:1 for Reels cross-post)
- On-screen copy do's and don'ts (emoji allowed, medical claims not)
- Music guidance (library link or "please use original sound")
- Deliverables (raw footage + edited cut, both in our Frame.io)

### Reposting
- **Always credit** the creator in the caption and on-screen (bottom-left, sans 10px, `@handle`).
- **Always add the HairMNL frame** — top bar with product name, bottom bar with CTA — drawn to the same spec as our reel covers. UGC doesn't get to break the template.
- **Never re-cut** a creator's video without their sign-off. Adding our frame and captioning is fine. Reordering shots is not.

---

## 5. Product flatlays (for retail + PDP)

- Paper or linen background only. Never marble, never acrylic.
- One shadow direction. The shadow falls **bottom-right** on every shelf flatlay so the grid reads as a system.
- **Product only, no hands** in the hero. Hands are fine in the lifestyle secondary.
- Liquids: decant into the real bottle; never fake the viscosity with Photoshop.
- The 3/4 angle is our default; top-down is reserved for routine-build grids where spatial relationships between products matter.

---

## 6. Talent &amp; representation

- **Filipino hair, first.** Our talent roster leads with Filipina/Filipino talent across hair types (1A–4C). When a brand partner pushes a global face, we do the shoot here, with our people.
- **Hair types in proportion.** We don't over-index on type 1 hair just because product shoots feel easier. A rolling 90-day content audit should show the full type range.
- **Ages 20–55.** Not just Gen Z. The brand serves its founders, and they are 40+.
- **Hair in motion.** We prefer talent who move — walking, turning, laughing mid-shot. Posed fashion-catalog shots are reserved for campaigns.

---

## 7. Post-production LUT

Every HairMNL edit runs through the same two-node grade:
1. **Warm neutral** — pull 2% warmth, 3% saturation out of cyans, lift shadows 4% with a warm tint.
2. **Grain** — 6–8% film grain, organic (no regular digital noise).

We don't use Instagram's in-app filters. We don't use TikTok's colour effects. Upload fully-graded.

---

## 8. What we won't shoot

- Top-down bathroom sink shots.
- Before/afters with obvious lighting changes (warm → cool, close → wide). If we're showing a transformation, both frames are lit and graded the same.
- "Serious before, happy after" acted shots. Condescending to the customer.
- Product shots on seamless white. White is not our colour. Paper is.
- Shiny vinyl textures, wet glass, dramatic macro droplets. Reads as commercial, not editorial.
